Four domains, one certification

The CISM exam covers four domains. Understanding the weight of each domain helps you prioritise your revision time effectively.

1

Information Security Governance

Establish and maintain a framework to ensure that the information security strategy is aligned with organizational goals and objectives.

Security Strategy Governance Frameworks Policy Hierarchy Roles & Accountability KPIs / KRIs / KGIs Reporting to Board

Identify and manage information security risks to achieve business objectives.

Exam Overview

Know before you sit

Key facts about the CISM examination administered by ISACA.

150 Questions
4 hrs Exam Duration
450 Passing Score
800 Maximum Score
🎯
Business-first thinking
CISM questions reward business-aligned thinking. Always prefer answers that connect security to business objectives over purely technical solutions.
⏱
Time management
With 150 questions in 4 hours, you have roughly 96 seconds per question. Flag uncertain questions and return — don't get stuck.
📌
Risk acceptance rule
Whenever a question involves accepting risk, the answer is always the business owner or senior management — never the CISO or security team.
🔑
"First" question pattern
Questions asking "what should be done FIRST" almost always require understanding current state or aligning to business strategy before taking action.
📋
Domain 3 is largest
Information Security Program accounts for 33% of the exam. Spend proportionally more revision time here — roughly 1 in 3 questions will come from this domain.
🌐
Outsourcing ≠ transfer of accountability
A frequently tested concept: outsourcing transfers financial liability but not accountability. Your organisation remains responsible to regulators and customers.

About this site

Built to help you pass

This training hub was built to support CISM candidates through structured, exam-focused preparation. All content is aligned to the current ISACA CISM exam domains and job practice areas.

The materials here cover what you need to know for the exam — especially the concepts that trip candidates up most often: risk acceptance authority, governance vs management, quantitative formulas, and the BCP/DRP/incident response hierarchies.

Use the mock exam repeatedly, review your weakest domains in the revision notes, and download the advanced study guide for deep dives into the hardest topics.

Study Recommendation
Start with the revision notes, then attempt the mock exam cold to find your weak domains. Use the advanced study guide to reinforce difficult concepts, then re-sit the mock exam to track your improvement.
