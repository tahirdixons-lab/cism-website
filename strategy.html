The "FIRST" Question Pattern

Many CISM questions ask what you should do first. There is almost always a pattern. Memorise these.

Responding to an active incidentContain — stop the spread
A major vulnerability is foundAssess the risk and business impact

The Risk Decision Authority Rule

Who decides what
  • Board / Senior Management: approve risk appetite, accept high residual risks, approve security policy
  • Business / Data Owner: accept residual risk for their specific assets and processes
  • CISM / CISO: recommend treatment, develop policy, manage programme — NOT final decision-maker
  • IT / Security Team: implement controls as directed — NOT risk acceptance authority
Golden Rule
  • Risk acceptance is always a business decision. If an answer has the CISM or security team accepting risk, it is almost certainly wrong.

Incident Response — Order Is Everything

Most Common Exam Error
  • Jumping from Detection straight to Eradication — skipping Containment. Always contain before eradicating.
#PhaseKey Point
1PreparationIRP must exist before any incident occurs
2Detection & AnalysisConfirm the incident is real; classify severity
3ContainmentSTOP THE SPREAD first — isolate affected systems
4EradicationRemove malware/root cause after containing
5RecoveryRestore and verify; monitor closely
6Lessons LearnedBlameless; update IRP; within 2 weeks

Five Rules to Apply to Every Question

Pre-answer checklist
  • Is this governance (Board) or management (CISO)? Don't mix them up.
  • Is there a "current state assessment" or "align to business" option? It's probably correct.
  • Does the question involve risk acceptance? Answer = business owner, not security team.
  • Is outsourcing involved? Transfers liability, not accountability.
  • Is there a more business-aligned answer and a more technical answer? Business wins.
